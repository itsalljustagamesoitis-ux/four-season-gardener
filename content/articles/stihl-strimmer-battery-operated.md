If you've been searching for a Stihl strimmer battery operated option and keep running into the same wall , Stihl's battery lineup isn't broadly available through Amazon in the US market , you're not imagining things. The FSA 57, FSA 86, and FSA 90 R are genuinely good machines, but getting your hands on one typically means going through a dealer, and that's before you factor in the cost of buying into Stihl's AK or AP battery system separately. For a lot of buyers, that's a reasonable trade-off. For many others, it isn't.

The [EGO POWER+ ST1521S 15" String Trimmer with POWERLOAD, 56V 2.5Ah Kit](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07BBW8P9Y?tag=fourseasong-20) is where I'd point most people instead. It's available now, it ships with battery and charger, and it comes from a brand that has built one of the more coherent [battery-powered cordless tool ecosystems](/battery-equipment/) in the outdoor power category. Whether it's worth your money depends on what you're actually cutting and how much of it. Here's my assessment.

## Quick Verdict

The EGO ST1521S is a capable, well-engineered battery trimmer that outperforms most of what's in its price range. The POWERLOAD automatic line-feed system is a real feature, not a marketing claim. The 56V 2.5Ah battery included with this kit delivers meaningful run time for a single session on a typical residential property. The proprietary line cartridge requirement will irritate some buyers.

Currently around $199 on Amazon for the kit (battery and rapid charger included), it competes directly against the Husqvarna 115iL and the Ryobi 40V PCO300, both of which I've used. The EGO is the better machine. I'd buy it again.

## Key Specs

The ST1521S runs on EGO's 56V ARC Lithium platform, which is the same battery that powers every EGO tool from their blowers to their mowers. The 2.5Ah pack in this kit is a medium-capacity battery in their lineup. It's not the biggest (that's the 7.5Ah), but it's a reasonable starting point and weighs considerably less.

![Stihl Strimmer Battery Operated](/images/articles/battery-equipment-5.jpg)


Cutting width is 15 inches. The shaft splits in two for storage and transport, which matters more than it sounds if you're working out of a truck bed or a tight shed. The motor is brushless, which extends motor life and improves efficiency compared to brushed alternatives.

Weight with battery is approximately 11.3 lbs. That's not light, but it's balanced well enough that the weight isn't punishing over a long session.

Line diameter is .095-inch using EGO's proprietary POWERLOAD cartridges.

## Performance and Testing

### Cutting Power

On established grass and light weeds, the ST1521S handles itself cleanly. I tested it along fence lines, around raised bed borders, and through a patch of overgrown orchard grass that I'd let go about three weeks longer than I should have (which I recognize is a specific complaint about my own management decisions, not the trimmer). It got through all of it without bogging down.

What it is not is a brush-clearing machine. Thick-stemmed woody growth above about 3/8-inch diameter will slow it down. For that kind of work, you'd want to be looking at EGO's heavier ST1623T, or honestly, a gas machine. The ST1521S is a lawn maintenance tool, not a land management tool.

### POWERLOAD System

This is the feature I was most skeptical about and ended up most impressed by. The POWERLOAD system uses pre-wound line cartridges. You open the head, drop in the cartridge, close it, and hold the trigger while tapping the bump head against the ground. The trimmer winds the line automatically in about five seconds.

If you've ever stood in the middle of a job with dead line, hands full of spool, trying to thread .095 cord through a tiny eyelet while sweating, that's what this eliminates. The cartridges run around $7 to $9 each and each one gives you roughly 20 to 25 feet of line per strand. On my property, I go through about one cartridge per two sessions.

![Stihl Strimmer Battery Operated](/images/articles/battery-equipment-6.jpg)


The catch: you cannot use bulk string. You are committed to EGO's cartridges, and if your local Home Depot is out, you're ordering online. Some buyers find this genuinely annoying. I find it a fair trade for never losing twenty minutes to a spool tangle mid-job.

### Battery and Run Time

With the 2.5Ah battery at full charge, I consistently got between 35 and 45 minutes of working run time at medium load settings. (I timed this over four sessions.) That's enough to handle approximately three-quarters of an acre of trim work, depending on density. For larger properties, you'd want to invest in a second battery or step up to the 5.0Ah or 7.5Ah pack.

EGO's battery platform is one of the reasons I'd lean toward this brand over a one-off purchase. That same 56V battery runs in EGO's mowers, blowers, and hedge trimmers. If you're already in the EGO ecosystem or planning to be, the battery investment starts making more sense across tools. I've covered the broader range of cordless outdoor tools on the [Battery & Cordless Tools](/battery-equipment/) hub if you're building out from scratch.

### Vibration and Handling

The carbon fiber split shaft is a genuine ergonomic improvement over the aluminum shafts in this price range. Vibration is noticeably lower than what I experienced with the Husqvarna 115iL over extended sessions. For anyone who deals with hand fatigue or has any history of tendinitis, that difference becomes significant after 20 minutes of continuous use.

![Stihl Strimmer Battery Operated](/images/articles/battery-equipment-7.jpg)


The auxiliary handle adjusts to three positions, which accommodates different user heights reasonably well. I'm 5'8" and found the default ergonomic position workable without adjustment. Taller users may find the mid-position handle more comfortable.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros**

- POWERLOAD cartridge system makes line replacement fast, clean, and genuinely foolproof
- 56V brushless motor handles dense grass and light overgrowth without throttling back
- Carbon fiber shaft reduces vibration meaningfully over longer sessions
- Complete kit at around $199 means no separate battery purchase required
- Same battery platform as the full EGO lineup, including their [pole hedge trimmer](https://fourseasongardener.com/ego-pole-hedge-trimmer/) and blowers

**Cons**

- Proprietary POWERLOAD cartridges only. No bulk string compatibility
- 15-inch cutting width is narrower than gas trimmers in this power class
- 2.5Ah battery is adequate for smaller properties but will leave larger jobs unfinished on a single charge
- Cartridge cost adds up over a full season of heavy use

## Who It's For

The ST1521S is well-matched to a homeowner maintaining somewhere between a half-acre and an acre of lawn and garden trim work per session. It's a strong fit if you're already in the EGO battery system or thinking about building one out. The POWERLOAD system particularly earns its keep if you're the type who finds detailed mechanical fussing (line routing, spool winding) disproportionately irritating relative to the actual task.

It's also a sensible first battery trimmer for someone coming off a gas machine who doesn't want to commit to the Stihl dealer channel. Stihl's battery tools, including the FSA 86 and FSA 90 R, are fine machines with a strong service network behind them. But if you want a brushless 56V trimmer with a complete kit available through normal retail channels today, the EGO is the practical answer.

![Stihl Strimmer Battery Operated](/images/articles/battery-equipment-8.jpg)


If your interest goes beyond string trimmers, the same logic applies to [battery-powered edgers](/stihl-battery-edger/) and even heavier tools like the [battery chainsaw category](/battery-stihl-chainsaw/), where the ecosystem argument for staying within a single platform becomes even stronger.

This isn't the right tool if you're managing rough terrain, clearing overgrown field margins, or dealing with heavy brush on a regular basis. For that kind of work, you need more torque, a heavier line, and probably a harness. The ST1521S is a precision maintenance tool, and it's priced and built accordingly.

Buyers who want absolute flexibility in line diameter and brand have a fair grievance with the POWERLOAD system. If that matters to you, the EGO ST1511 is the standard spool version at slightly lower cost, though you give up the auto-wind feature. For most buyers, my read is that the cartridge convenience wins, but I recognize that's not everyone's calculation.

One final point: if you're also in the market for a leaf blower, the 40V cordless options are worth comparing against EGO's 56V blowers before you commit to a battery platform. I covered the case for and against 40V systems in the [40V cordless leaf blower](/40v-cordless-leaf-blower/) review, which is relevant context if you're building out a cordless tool set rather than buying a single trimmer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is the EGO ST1521S a good replacement for a Stihl FSA 86 or FSA 90 R battery trimmer?

For most residential applications, yes. The Stihl FSA 86 and FSA 90 R are slightly more powerful at the top end and benefit from Stihl's dealer service network, but they're not available through major online retailers in kit form. The EGO ST1521S ships with battery and charger at around $199 and performs comparably on standard lawn trim work. If you need dealer support or already own Stihl AK/AP batteries, the Stihl route may make more sense for you.

![Stihl Strimmer Battery Operated](/images/articles/battery-equipment-1.jpg)


### Can I use any brand of trimmer line with the EGO ST1521S?

No. The POWERLOAD head on the ST1521S accepts only EGO's proprietary pre-wound line cartridges. These run approximately $7 to $9 per cartridge and are available at Home Depot and online. If bulk string compatibility is important to you, consider the EGO ST1511, which uses a conventional bump-feed spool and accepts standard .095 trimmer line.

### How long does the battery last on a single charge?

With the included 2.5Ah battery at medium load, expect 35 to 45 minutes of working run time. That's enough for most residential lots under an acre. Heavier growth or continuous full-throttle use will reduce that. EGO's 5.0Ah and 7.5Ah batteries fit the same tool and extend run time proportionally.

### Does the EGO 56V battery work with other EGO tools?

Yes. EGO's 56V ARC Lithium battery platform is shared across their entire product lineup, including mowers, blowers, hedge trimmers, and chainsaws. The 2.5Ah battery included with this kit will work in any current EGO 56V tool, though run time will vary by the tool's power draw.

### Is the EGO ST1521S suitable for large properties or commercial use?

Not ideally. It's built for residential maintenance use on properties up to roughly an acre per charge. Commercial operators or those managing larger acreage would be better served by a higher-capacity battery setup, a dual-line head, or a gas trimmer with the torque to handle sustained heavy-duty work. The ST1521S does its specific job well, but its specific job is residential lawn care.
