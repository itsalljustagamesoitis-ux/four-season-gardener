If you're shopping for solar powered garden lights for sale and you want a straight answer about which product actually fits your situation, this comparison is for you. The market is full of solar motion lights that promise a lot and deliver inconsistently, so I'm going to focus on two products I've spent real time evaluating: the [Mr Beams Solar Wedge Plus 102 LED Security Outdoor Wall Light](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07W464JPY?tag=fourseasong-20) and the [LITOM 30 LED Solar Motion Sensor Landscape Spotlights, 4-Pack](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B075GQ54CY?tag=fourseasong-20). They're both budget-range options. They're not trying to do the same job. And one of them is probably the right answer for your property.

For broader context on how these fit into a full outdoor setup, the [Garden Lighting](/lighting/) hub is a useful starting point if you're still mapping out your approach.

## At-a-Glance

The Mr Beams Solar Wedge Plus is a self-contained wall light. The solar panel is built into the unit itself, mounted on top. You screw it to a wall, aim it, and you're done. No separate panel, no cable routing, no decisions about panel placement. It puts out 410 lumens from 102 LEDs, which is meaningfully brighter than most solar motion lights in this price range. At the time of writing, it's available for around $30 to $35 on Amazon.

The LITOM 30 LED Solar Motion Sensor Landscape Spotlights come as a 4-pack, currently around $40 to $45, which works out to roughly $10 to $11 per unit. Each fixture has a separate solar panel on a stake, so you can angle the panel toward the sun independently of where the light head points. They carry an IP67 waterproof rating, which is the spec that distinguishes them from the field.

Neither product integrates with smart home systems. Neither has a replaceable battery. Both require direct sun exposure to charge reliably. Those are the constraints. Everything else is a question of what you're actually trying to light.

## Why Choose the Mr Beams Solar Wedge Plus

This is the right product if you want a single motion-activated security light on a wall and you want the installation to take ten minutes with a screwdriver.

The built-in solar panel is the defining feature. Most solar security lights separate the panel from the fixture, which means you're mounting two things and running a cable between them. The Wedge Plus eliminates that entirely. The panel sits flush on top of the housing, the whole unit goes on the wall, and that's the project. For anyone renting, or anyone who doesn't want to commit to a more permanent installation, that matters. If you've ever looked at a wired option like the LEONLITE hardwired security light and decided the electrical work isn't worth it for one corner of a garage, the Wedge Plus is the direct alternative.

The 410-lumen output is worth calling out specifically, because solar motion lights at this price are usually in the 200 to 300 lumen range. That difference is visible. The light actually covers a driveway entrance or a back gate rather than just suggesting coverage.

Motion sensitivity is the primary mode. The light stays off, activates on motion, runs for a set duration, then shuts off. There's no permanent-on option, which is a real limitation if you want path lighting rather than security lighting. This is a deterrent and a convenience light, not a landscape light.

The placement constraint is the honest caveat. The panel is on the unit itself, which means the unit has to be in direct sun. On a wall that faces the right direction, this is fine. On a north-facing wall or a heavily shaded spot, the unit won't charge adequately. That's not a design flaw so much as the physics of a self-contained solar product. Worth verifying your wall orientation before ordering. (I've seen people return these after one cloudy week when the real issue was a wall that faces northeast and loses sun by 2 p.m.)

## Why Choose the LITOM 30 LED Solar Motion Sensor Landscape Spotlights

The LITOM pack solves a different problem. If you have a front pathway, a garden border, a set of steps, or any continuous run that needs multiple lights, buying four units at once for under $45 is a different value calculation than buying individual wall-mount fixtures.

The IP67 waterproof rating is the technical standout. Most solar lights in this price bracket are rated IP65, which means protection against water jets. IP67 means the units can be submerged in up to one meter of water for 30 minutes. In practical terms, this matters during heavy spring rain, standing water situations, or ground areas that flood temporarily. If your property gets real weather, and by real weather I mean the kind that pools around stakes and floods low spots overnight, IP67 is a meaningful upgrade over IP65 in a way that IP65 versus IP44 is not.

The three lighting modes are genuinely useful for a landscape application. Mode one is motion-activated, which works like any security light. Mode two is permanent-on at low brightness, suitable for ambient path lighting all night. Mode three is permanent-on at full brightness, which drains the battery faster but provides consistent output if you have a specific reason to need it. The flexibility to run the same fixture in different modes depending on the location is something the Mr Beams unit simply doesn't offer.

![Solar Powered Garden Lights For Sale](/images/articles/lighting-8.jpg)


Because the solar panel is on a separate stake, you can optimize panel placement independently of the light head direction. If you're lighting a north-facing bed, you can stake the panel out to the south side while angling the light head into the border. That's a real advantage in situations where the Mr Beams' integrated design would fail entirely.

The battery limitation is worth stating plainly. The battery is built-in and not replaceable. Lithium batteries in outdoor solar lights typically degrade noticeably after two to four years of charge cycles. At roughly $10 per unit, the replacement cost is low enough that this probably doesn't warrant serious concern, but if you're expecting a five-year or ten-year product, recalibrate. These are consumables at this price point, not infrastructure.

## Verdict

Buy the [Mr Beams Solar Wedge Plus](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07W464JPY?tag=fourseasong-20) if you need one good motion-activated light on a wall and you want installation that's genuinely tool-optional. It's brighter than the competition at this price, the self-contained design removes the cable and separate panel problem, and it does the security light job well. It won't work in a shaded location, and it's not a path light. But if a wall mount facing the sun is what you have, it's hard to beat for simplicity and output.

Buy the [LITOM 30 LED Solar Motion Sensor Landscape Spotlights, 4-Pack](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B075GQ54CY?tag=fourseasong-20) if you're covering a pathway, a garden border, or any multi-point installation where you need four lights and you want IP67 protection plus mode flexibility. The per-unit cost is lower, the waterproofing is better than most products at this price, and the separate panel stakes give you positioning freedom the Wedge Plus can't match. The non-replaceable battery is a real limitation over a long time horizon, but at this price, the useful life is acceptable.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long do solar garden lights actually last at night?

At full charge, most solar motion lights in this price range run for six to eight hours of total on-time, assuming intermittent activation rather than continuous output. Continuous modes drain batteries much faster. In practice, across a winter with shorter days and less sun, expect reduced run times. The LITOM's low-brightness permanent-on mode is more forgiving of a partial charge than its full-brightness mode.

### Are solar garden lights worth buying for a shaded yard?

Honestly, no, not in deeply shaded conditions. Solar lights need direct sun for several hours per day to charge adequately. Partial shade (two to three hours of direct sun) produces reduced but usable performance. Full shade makes both of these products unreliable. The LITOM's separate panel stake helps in partial shade situations, since you can position the panel in a sunnier spot. The Mr Beams integrated design gives you no flexibility on this.

![Solar Powered Garden Lights For Sale](/images/articles/lighting-2.jpg)


### What does IP67 waterproof rating mean for garden lights?

IP67 means the unit is rated to withstand submersion in up to one meter of water for 30 minutes. For outdoor garden use, this is higher protection than the more common IP65 rating, which covers water jets but not submersion. IP67 matters if lights are placed in low-lying areas that pool water or in regions with heavy, sustained rain. For wall-mounted lights well above ground, the difference between IP65 and IP67 is less relevant in practice.

### Can I replace the batteries in these solar lights?

Not in either of these products. Both the Mr Beams Solar Wedge Plus and the LITOM spotlights use built-in rechargeable batteries that are not designed for user replacement. Battery degradation after several years of charge cycles is the expected failure mode for both. At the price points of these units (roughly $30 to $35 for the Mr Beams, $10 to $11 per unit for the LITOM), replacement of the full fixture is the practical approach when performance declines noticeably.

### Do either of these solar lights work with Alexa or Google Home?

No. Neither the Mr Beams Solar Wedge Plus nor the LITOM 30 LED spotlights offer any smart home connectivity. They operate on their own built-in sensors and mode settings only. If smart home integration is a priority, you're looking at a different product category, typically hardwired or plug-in fixtures with Wi-Fi or Zigbee connectivity, which carry a higher price and require a different installation approach. The [outdoor light security camera](/outdoor-light-security-camera/) category covers some combination units that include connectivity options if that's the direction you want to go.
