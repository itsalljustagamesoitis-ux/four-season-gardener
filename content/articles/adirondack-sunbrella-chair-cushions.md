If your Adirondack chairs are sound but the cushions look like they've been through three bad summers, replacing the fabric is a smarter move than replacing the furniture. Good outdoor frames last decades. Cushion fabric, depending on what it's made from, often doesn't. The [RSH Decor Sunbrella Deep Seating Replacement Cushion with Back, 25"x25"](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B08JCJKMT9?tag=fourseasong-20) sits squarely in the category of cushions worth buying once and not thinking about again for a long time. Whether that's true in practice is what this review is about.

For broader context on what to look for before buying any replacement cushion, the site's [Outdoor Furniture](/outdoor-furniture/) hub is a reasonable starting point, particularly if you're also weighing whether the frame itself needs attention.

## Quick Verdict

This is a well-made replacement cushion set using genuine Sunbrella solution-dyed acrylic fabric, which is the material standard serious outdoor furniture buyers have used for years. At around $85 to $120 depending on color and fill option at the time of writing, it's not cheap for a cushion. It's also not overpriced for what you're getting. The main thing to get right before ordering is your measurements. Get those wrong and you'll be dealing with a return on something bulky and awkward to repackage.

**Buy it if:** Your chair frame is in good shape and you want a cushion that holds up to rain, sun, and mildew without annual replacement rituals.

**Skip it if:** You're outfitting new chairs and haven't decided on a frame yet. Start with the frame decision first, then come back to cushioning.

The RSH Decor cushion set covers both the seat and back, sized at 25" x 25". Sunbrella fabric is solution-dyed, meaning the color runs through the fiber rather than sitting on top of it, which is why it doesn't fade the way polyester-covered cushions do after a season of direct sun.

## Performance and Testing

### Fabric Durability

Sunbrella has been the reference point for outdoor upholstery fabric for long enough that comparing it to standard polyester covers feels almost beside the point. I've had Sunbrella cushions on a pair of teak chairs since 2019, and the color difference between a cushion that's been in direct afternoon sun and one that spent most of its time in shade is minimal. Polyester covers from the same period on a different set lost their color noticeably by season two.

The RSH Decor cushions use genuine Sunbrella fabric, which matters because there are knockoff "solution-dyed acrylic" products that don't hold up the same way. If you've read anything on this site about [Sunbrella Adirondack chair cushions](/sunbrella-adirondack-chair-cushions/), you'll know the fabric spec is the first thing worth verifying before buying any cushion marketed with "Sunbrella-style" language.

Mold and mildew resistance is real, not marketing. Left damp after rain, Sunbrella fabric dries faster than polyester and doesn't develop the gray-green surface mildew you see on cheaper cushions by mid-summer. That said, if you're jamming wet cushions into a storage bin and closing the lid, mold will find a way regardless of what the fabric is made from.

![Adirondack Sunbrella Chair Cushions](/images/articles/outdoor-furniture-7.jpg)


### Fit and Sizing

This is where I'd slow down if I were buying these for the first time. The 25" x 25" spec describes the cushion face dimensions, not necessarily how it will sit in your specific chair. Adirondack chairs are not manufactured to a single standard. A 25" seat cushion on a wide-slatted chair will fit differently than the same cushion on a narrower profile.

Measure your existing cushion, not your chair seat. If your current cushion is 24" x 23", you're looking at a custom or different-size order. RSH Decor does offer other dimensions, so the 25" x 25" isn't your only option, but it's the most commonly stocked size. (I cannot stress "measure first" strongly enough for anyone who has ever tried to return a large cushion through the mail.)

### Fill Performance

The quick-dry polyester fill drains faster after rain and resumes its shape better over a season of use than standard polyester fill. High-density foam is firmer and holds its loft longer, but it also retains moisture more than the quick-dry option. For chairs that live outside uncovered for the whole season, quick-dry is the more practical call.

### Construction Quality

Stitching on the RSH Decor cushions is tight and even. The zipper closure on the cover allows for washing, which you'll want to do once or twice a season regardless of how durable Sunbrella is. Cleaning Sunbrella is straightforward: mild soap, water, rinse well, air dry. That process works and doesn't degrade the fabric.

The back cushion attachment method is worth checking when yours arrives. Some configurations use ties, some use velcro, and the degree to which your back cushion stays put depends partly on the slat spacing of your chair. On chairs with wider spacing between back slats, thin tie attachments can shift. Not a dealbreaker, but worth adjusting expectations accordingly, which I realize is a specific complaint that may only apply to older chair frames.

## Who It's For

If you own Adirondack chairs that are structurally sound but cosmetically tired, this is the obvious move. Quality wood or poly-resin frames built by reputable manufacturers are designed to last 15 to 20 years or more. Replacing cushions at year four or five is not failure; it's normal maintenance. Buying a Sunbrella replacement cushion set is considerably cheaper than buying a new chair.

This cushion also works well on sectional pieces and loveseats beyond Adirondack frames, if you're in a position of refreshing a larger seating area rather than individual chairs. Anyone who's looked at our coverage of the [teak outdoor rocking chair](/teak-outdoor-rocking-chair/) or [teak outdoor dining set](/teak-outdoor-dining-set/) will recognize that the approach is the same: invest in the durable frame once, treat cushions as the component you'll refresh on a longer but still finite schedule.

![Adirondack Sunbrella Chair Cushions](/images/articles/outdoor-furniture-1.jpg)


For shoppers who have never used Sunbrella fabric before and are wondering whether the price premium is real or aspirational, I'd say it's real. The difference between a $40 polyester cushion and a $100 Sunbrella cushion isn't visible in the first season. It becomes visible in the second and third. If you're replacing cushions every two years because of fading and mildew, the economics actually favor Sunbrella over time.

One situation where I'd pause: if your chair frame itself is wobbling, cracking, or showing structural fatigue, spending $100 on new cushions for it is misallocating money. Evaluate the frame honestly before buying. If the frame has another decade in it, the cushions are worth the investment.

These cushions are also appropriate for anyone who wants an outdoor seating setup that doesn't require weekly interventions. If you have ever pulled out cushions on a Monday morning and spent twenty minutes scrubbing mildew off before guests arrive on Saturday, the upgrade to Sunbrella fabric is simply the right call.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are these cushions actually made with genuine Sunbrella fabric?

Yes. RSH Decor uses genuine Sunbrella solution-dyed acrylic fabric, not a generic acrylic alternative. You can verify this because Sunbrella is a branded material manufactured by Glen Raven, and RSH Decor's product listing specifies this. If you're ever uncertain about a cushion listing, look for the Sunbrella name explicitly rather than phrases like "Sunbrella-style" or "outdoor acrylic," which don't mean the same thing.

### How do I clean these cushions?

Sunbrella fabric cleans well with mild soap (dish soap works), water, and a soft brush. Apply the solution, let it sit briefly, scrub gently, and rinse thoroughly. The key failure mode with Sunbrella cleaning is not rinsing completely. Soap residue left in the fabric can attract dirt and eventually affect the fabric's performance. Air dry completely before storing or covering.

![Adirondack Sunbrella Chair Cushions](/images/articles/outdoor-furniture-2.jpg)


### Will the 25"x25" size fit my Adirondack chair?

It depends on your chair. Adirondack chairs are not manufactured to a single dimension standard. Measure your existing cushion before ordering, not the chair seat itself. If your current cushion is smaller than 25"x25", this cushion may overhang the seat. RSH Decor offers other dimensions, so check the full size range if 25"x25" doesn't match your measurement.

### What's the difference between the fill options?

Standard polyester is the baseline. It's serviceable but will compress over time and retains moisture longer after rain. High-density foam holds its shape and loft better over multiple seasons but is slower to dry. Quick-dry polyester is the middle option: reasonable shape retention and significantly faster drying after rain, which makes it the practical choice for chairs that stay outside uncovered. If your chairs are under a covered porch or you always use furniture covers, the fill difference matters less.

### Is it worth replacing cushions instead of buying a new chair?

For a solid wood or poly-resin Adirondack frame in good structural condition, yes. Quality frames are built to outlast multiple cushion cycles by a significant margin. A replacement Sunbrella cushion set at $85 to $120 versus a new quality Adirondack chair at $300 to $600 or more is not a close comparison if the frame still has years in it. The calculation changes if the frame itself is failing, in which case directing budget toward a better frame makes more sense than cushioning a chair that won't last.
