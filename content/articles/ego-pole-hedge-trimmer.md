The [EGO POWER+ HT2411 24" Cordless Hedge Trimmer](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0799QY4TW?tag=fourseasong-20) sits at the lower end of EGO's hedge trimmer lineup in terms of price, but it runs on the same 56V Arc Lithium battery platform as their premium tools. That matters if you're already running EGO equipment or considering building out a battery-powered yard setup. I've been testing cordless outdoor tools across my 12-acre property for several years now, and I have a reasonably clear picture of what separates the honest performers from the ones that look good in spec sheets. This trimmer is mostly the former, with a few caveats worth spelling out before you hand over money.

If you're comparing cordless options more broadly, the [Battery & Cordless Tools](/battery-equipment/) hub on this site covers the EGO platform and its competitors in more depth.

## Quick Verdict

The HT2411 is a capable, well-built hedge trimmer at a price point that's hard to argue with. The kit, which includes a 2.5Ah battery and standard charger, currently runs around $149 to $169 on Amazon depending on timing. For a new EGO user, that's reasonable entry into a battery platform you'll actually want to stay in. The 24-inch blade handles most residential hedging work without complaint, and the 1-inch cut capacity is better than you typically get at this price.

The battery runtime is the honest limitation. Forty-five minutes of continuous use is about what you get, which covers a lot of hedges on a normal property but runs short if you have a serious amount of formal hedging to maintain. If that's your situation, you'll want to look at the extended-battery options covered in our [EGO hedge trimmer with battery and charger](/ego-hedge-trimmer-with-battery-and-charger/) piece before deciding.

**Bottom line.** Buy it if you have moderate hedging needs and want to get into the EGO platform without spending $250. Skip it if your hedging takes longer than 45 minutes in a single session and you don't want to wait for a recharge mid-job.

## Key Specs

- **Blade length.** 24 inches
- **Cut capacity.** 1 inch
- **Motor type.** Brushless
- **Strokes per minute.** 3,000
- **Battery included.** 56V 2.5Ah Arc Lithium
- **Weight.** 7.3 lbs (with battery)
- **Blade type.** Dual-action hardened steel
- **Warranty.** 5 years on tool, 3 years on battery

The brushless motor is worth flagging specifically. At this price, some competitors still ship brushed motors, which run hotter, wear faster, and deliver less consistent power through thick material. The HT2411's brushless design puts it ahead of options like the Black+Decker LHT2436B, which comes in at a similar price but with a brushed motor and meaningfully less cut capacity.

## Performance and Testing

### Blade Performance on Typical Hedge Material

I ran the HT2411 through boxwood, privet, and a section of overgrown forsythia that I probably should have dealt with last year. The 1-inch cut capacity is not marketing language. It genuinely handles branches at that diameter without bogging down, which matters for forsythia and any hedge that's been left to get ahead of you. On the boxwood and privet, the dual-action blades produce a clean finish without the tearing you sometimes see on single-action cutters at lower speeds.

At 3,000 strokes per minute, the blade moves fast enough that you're not fighting the tool through material. The dual-action design also reduces vibration noticeably compared to single-action alternatives, which becomes relevant over a full session. If you've ever finished a hedge trimming job with your forearms buzzing for an hour afterward, that's specifically what the dual-action mechanism reduces.

![Ego Pole Hedge Trimmer](/images/articles/battery-equipment-7.jpg)


### Battery Life in Real Conditions

Forty-five minutes is accurate under normal use, meaning light to medium hedge material with occasional pauses. Push it through dense overgrown material continuously and you'll likely see closer to 35 minutes. That's not a failure mode, it's just physics. The 2.5Ah battery is adequate for the tool's intended use case.

The 56V platform means that if you already own a larger EGO battery, say the 5.0Ah or 7.5Ah pack, you can drop it into this trimmer and extend your runtime substantially. The 7.5Ah battery currently runs around $179 separately, which obviously changes the economics, but it's worth knowing the option exists. (I've been running the 5.0Ah across multiple EGO tools and it's the battery I'd buy first if you're equipping from scratch.)

### Handling and Weight

Seven point three pounds with battery installed is noticeably lighter than a comparable gas trimmer. The Husqvarna 122HD45, which I ran for several seasons before switching to battery tools, weighs in at around 9.9 lbs and adds fatigue from vibration on top of that. Over 45 minutes of overhead or extended-reach work, the weight difference matters more than it sounds on paper.

The one handling complaint I have is specific to overhead cuts. The blade guard, which is a sensible safety feature, makes one-handed positioning awkward when you're reaching up into the top of a taller hedge. Both hands on the tool is the correct posture anyway, but the guard catches on branches when you're maneuvering at angle. This is a design choice I understand but find mildly irritating in practice (which I realize is a specific complaint to make about a hedge trimmer).

![Ego Pole Hedge Trimmer](/images/articles/battery-equipment-8.jpg)


### Noise and Vibration

Quieter than any gas trimmer I've used, which is both accurate and unsurprising. The dual-action blades keep vibration manageable through a full session. Nothing remarkable to report here, just solid execution.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros**

- 1-inch cut capacity handles woody and overgrown material better than most cordless options at this price
- Brushless motor means longer tool lifespan and more consistent power delivery
- At 7.3 lbs, noticeably lighter than gas alternatives
- Dual-action hardened steel blades reduce both vibration and tearing on cut surfaces
- Kit includes battery and charger, good value as an entry point into the EGO 56V platform
- Five-year tool warranty is better than the category average

**Cons**

- 2.5Ah battery limits continuous runtime to around 45 minutes, potentially less in heavy material
- Blade guard creates handling awkwardness on overhead cuts at angle
- No variable speed control; single speed only
- Not the right choice if your hedging regularly runs beyond one battery charge and you don't want to invest in a larger battery

## Who It's For

The HT2411 is a practical choice for homeowners with a moderate amount of formal or informal hedging, a property where one charge covers the session, and either an existing EGO battery investment or the intent to build one. The kit price makes it a low-risk entry point into the EGO platform, especially if you're also running or considering an EGO mower or blower. The same 56V battery that powers this trimmer works across EGO's full lineup, which is the real long-term argument for platform loyalty.

If you're evaluating a broader cordless yard setup, our coverage of [40V cordless leaf blowers](/40v-cordless-leaf-blower/) is worth reading alongside this, since the battery platform question is similar and the same logic applies.

![Ego Pole Hedge Trimmer](/images/articles/battery-equipment-1.jpg)


It's not the right tool if your hedging is genuinely extensive, say, a half-acre of formal hedgerow that takes well over an hour to work through. For that, you either need multiple batteries or a look at EGO's 24-inch model with a larger battery pack.

It's also not for anyone committed to gas. If you want the power headroom and don't mind the weight and maintenance, gas is still gas. But if you've been looking for a reason to switch, the HT2411 at this price is a reasonable one.

## A Note on Platform Value

One thing that sometimes gets underweighted in hedge trimmer reviews is what the tool costs in context of the battery ecosystem. Buying this kit at around $159 gets you a 56V battery and charger that work with EGO's mowers, blowers, chainsaws, and string trimmers. The battery alone retails for around $80 to $90 at 2.5Ah. So you're effectively paying around $70 to $80 for the trimmer itself once you factor that out. At that price, the hardware quality, motor type, and cut capacity are genuinely hard to beat.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does the battery last on the EGO HT2411?

With the included 2.5Ah battery, expect around 45 minutes of continuous use on light to medium hedge material. Dense or woody material will reduce that to around 35 minutes. The trimmer accepts any EGO 56V Arc Lithium battery, so running a 5.0Ah or 7.5Ah pack will extend runtime significantly.

![Ego Pole Hedge Trimmer](/images/articles/battery-equipment-2.jpg)


### Can I use my existing EGO batteries with the HT2411?

Yes. The HT2411 runs on the EGO 56V Arc Lithium platform, which is the same battery system used across EGO's full range of outdoor power equipment. If you own an EGO mower, blower, or chainsaw, the battery from that tool will work in this trimmer.

### Is the EGO HT2411 good for thick branches?

The 1-inch cut capacity handles most hedge material you'd encounter in a maintained residential setting, including older wood on forsythia, privet, and boxwood that's been left to grow out. It's not a pruning saw replacement, but for hedge trimming work it handles more than the typical cordless trimmer at this price point.

### How does the HT2411 compare to gas hedge trimmers?

At 7.3 lbs, the HT2411 is noticeably lighter than comparable gas models. The Husqvarna 122HD45, for reference, weighs around 9.9 lbs and adds vibration fatigue that compounds over a session. The cordless trimmer won't match the raw power headroom of a professional gas unit, but for residential hedging it covers the same ground without the noise, exhaust, or maintenance overhead.

### Does the EGO HT2411 come with a battery and charger?

Yes, the standard kit includes a 2.5Ah 56V battery and a standard charger. This is one of the reasons the kit represents good value as an entry point into the EGO platform. At around $149 to $169 at the time of writing, you're getting a functional battery and charger along with the trimmer, which makes the effective tool cost considerably lower than the sticker price suggests.
