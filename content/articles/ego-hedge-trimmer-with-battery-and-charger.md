I've been trimming the hedges on this property for close to fifteen years, and for most of that time I ran a Husqvarna 122HD45 gas trimmer. It worked. It also smelled, needed a carburetor clean every spring, and required a cord pull that would test the patience of someone half my age. When I started moving toward the EGO battery platform a few years ago, starting with their mower, the hedge trimmer was always the next logical step. The [EGO POWER+ HT2411 24" Cordless Hedge Trimmer](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0799QY4TW?tag=fourseasong-20) is the entry point to that category for EGO: kit-priced, battery and charger included, and marketed as a serious cutting tool rather than a weekend-warrior toy. I've been running it for two full seasons. Here's my read.

If you're building out a cordless tool lineup and want to understand where this fits in the broader picture, the site's [Battery & Cordless Tools](/battery-equipment/) section covers the EGO ecosystem in more depth, including comparisons across categories.

## Quick Verdict

The HT2411 is a competent hedge trimmer at a fair price. It cuts cleanly, the 1-inch branch capacity is real and not marketing fiction, and at 7 lbs it won't destroy your shoulders during a long session. The included 2.5Ah battery is the limiting factor, giving you roughly 45 minutes of continuous use before you're either waiting for a charge or reaching for a spare. If you already own EGO batteries from another tool, the kit price is slightly harder to justify. If you're coming in fresh, it's a reasonable way to start the platform.

**Recommended for:** New EGO users, properties with moderate hedge volume, anyone switching from gas who wants a direct comparison point.

**Not recommended for:** Larger properties where you'll be trimming for 90-plus minutes at a stretch without a break, or buyers who already own a 5Ah or 7.5Ah EGO battery and would rather buy the bare tool.

## Key Specs

The HT2411 runs on EGO's 56V Arc Lithium platform. The blade is 24 inches of dual-action hardened steel, cutting up to 1 inch in diameter. The brushless motor delivers 3,000 strokes per minute. Weight is 7 lbs without the battery, which adds roughly 1.3 lbs depending on pack size.

The kit (battery and charger included) runs around $130 to $150 on Amazon at the time of writing. The bare tool is available for closer to $90 to $100, which matters if you're already in the EGO ecosystem. The included charger is EGO's standard 56V unit, not the rapid charger, so expect around 50 minutes to recharge the 2.5Ah pack from empty.

A few numbers worth holding onto.

- Blade length: 24 inches
- Cut capacity: 1 inch diameter
- Strokes per minute: 3,000
- Battery included: 56V 2.5Ah (BA1120)
- Claimed run time: up to 75 minutes (EGO's figure, under optimal conditions)
- Actual run time in continuous cutting: 40 to 50 minutes (my experience)
- Weight with battery: approximately 8.3 lbs

## Performance and Testing

### Cutting Power

The 1-inch cut capacity claim held up. I have a run of privet hedge on the north side of the property that I've let get away from me in places, and there were stems in the 3/4-inch to 1-inch range I'd normally have gone after with loppers first. The HT2411 took them cleanly without bogging. The brushless motor doesn't have the same raw sound as a gas engine, but it doesn't lack for torque. If you've ever stalled a corded trimmer on a thick stem and had the blade bind, this doesn't do that in the same way. It slows, adjusts, and pushes through.

Boxwood and arborvitae, which make up the majority of my formal hedge work, cut smoothly and quickly. At 3,000 strokes per minute the blade moves fast enough that you're not having to linger. I ran it against a neighbor's Greenworks 24V 22-inch trimmer at one point, and the EGO's performance on denser material wasn't close. The Greenworks is fine for light maintenance cuts. The HT2411 is a different tool.

### Run Time: Honest Numbers

EGO's claimed 75-minute run time is based on light duty cutting, which I take to mean light maintenance on already-trimmed hedges. Under actual working conditions, cutting through established growth with stems in the half-inch range regularly, I got 40 to 45 minutes from the 2.5Ah pack. (I timed this, because the marketing figure struck me as optimistic and I wanted to know what I was actually working with.)

That's not necessarily a dealbreaker. Forty minutes of continuous hedge trimming is a reasonable work session, and if you're stopping periodically to clear clippings or reposition a ladder, the battery outlasts the session. The problem comes if you're doing a long hedge run without interruption. My privet hedge is roughly 180 linear feet and takes about an hour of steady cutting to do properly. With the 2.5Ah pack, I'm stopping once. With a 5Ah battery, I probably don't stop at all.

If you're going to run this trimmer on larger jobs, I'd budget for a second battery. The 5Ah pack (BA2800T) currently runs around $130 on its own, which adds meaningfully to the kit cost but solves the run time question entirely.

### Vibration and Fatigue

The dual-action blade design actually does reduce vibration compared to single-action alternatives, and this is worth talking about because it affects fatigue over a full session. My older Husqvarna gas trimmer had enough vibration that after 30 minutes my hands were noticeably tired. The HT2411 is markedly better. Not completely neutral, but better.

![Ego Hedge Trimmer With Battery And Charger](/images/articles/battery-equipment-4.jpg)


At 8.3 lbs with the battery, it's not light by cordless standards. If you've ever abandoned a blower mid-session because your forearm gave out, that's a relevant data point here. For overhead cuts especially, where you're holding the trimmer above your head and angling the blade, you'll feel the weight in your arms after about 15 minutes. The tool balances reasonably well, which helps, but there's no getting around the physics.

### The Blade Guard Issue

The blade guard is one area where the design doesn't fully think through practical use. It's present for obvious safety reasons and I'm not suggesting you remove it. On standard horizontal and vertical cuts it's a non-factor. On overhead cuts at an angle, particularly when you're trying to get underneath an overgrown section and cut upward, the guard catches awkwardly if you're trying to manage the trimmer one-handed while the other hand holds a branch out of the way. This is a specific complaint, I realize, and it won't come up in routine maintenance trimming. But it's real.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros**

- 1-inch cut capacity genuinely handles thick growth, not just soft new shoots
- Brushless motor at 3,000 SPM cuts quickly through dense hedges
- Lighter than comparable gas trimmers by a significant margin
- Dual-action blade cuts vibration noticeably, which matters over a long session
- Kit price is reasonable for new EGO users entering the platform
- 56V battery works across the full EGO lineup

**Cons**

- 2.5Ah battery gives 40 to 45 minutes of working run time, not 75
- Blade guard creates awkward geometry on one-handed overhead cuts
- Standard charger, not rapid charger, so recharging takes close to an hour
- Bare tool is better value if you already own EGO batteries

## Who It's For

If you're coming off gas and looking for a direct replacement without losing cutting capacity, this is the right comparison point. It won't do things the Husqvarna 122HD45 couldn't do, but it also won't demand carburetor cleaning, premixed fuel, or a functioning pull cord.

![Ego Hedge Trimmer With Battery And Charger](/images/articles/battery-equipment-5.jpg)


If you're already invested in the EGO 56V platform, maybe through their lawn equipment or their [40V Cordless Leaf Blower](/40v-cordless-leaf-blower/) category tools, the battery compatibility makes this a logical add. You're not buying into a new system. You're adding a tool to one you already own, and if you have larger batteries from that equipment, your run time problem goes away.

For a property with a modest amount of formal hedging, call it under 100 linear feet total, the 2.5Ah battery and the HT2411's cutting ability are well matched. For larger properties with more hedge volume, the trimmer is capable but plan to spend an extra $130 on a second battery, or look at the HT2410 bare tool paired with a 7.5Ah pack.

This isn't a tool for someone who wants to do the bare minimum. If your hedging needs are light, a less expensive cordless option would cover it. The HT2411 is built for people who are maintaining real hedges with real growth, and it's priced and speced to match that use case.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does the EGO HT2411 come with a battery and charger?

Yes. The kit includes a 56V 2.5Ah battery (model BA1120) and EGO's standard 56V charger. It's one of the things that makes the kit price reasonable for new EGO users. If you already own EGO batteries, the bare tool is available separately for around $90 to $100 and is the better buy.

![Ego Hedge Trimmer With Battery And Charger](/images/articles/battery-equipment-6.jpg)


### How long does the battery last on the EGO HT2411?

EGO's official figure is up to 75 minutes. In practice, cutting through established hedge growth with stems in the half-inch to 1-inch range, expect 40 to 45 minutes from the included 2.5Ah pack. Light maintenance cutting on already-trimmed hedges will get you closer to the manufacturer's number. If run time is a concern, a 5Ah battery extends working time significantly.

### Is the EGO HT2411 battery compatible with other EGO tools?

Yes. EGO's 56V Arc Lithium batteries are compatible across the full EGO Power+ lineup, including their mowers, blowers, chainsaws, and string trimmers. If you own other EGO equipment, any 56V battery you have will run the HT2411. This cross-compatibility is one of the stronger arguments for committing to the platform.

### Can the EGO HT2411 cut branches over 1 inch in diameter?

The rated cut capacity is 1 inch. In practice, the trimmer handles stems right at that limit without much difficulty on established hedge wood. Anything significantly over 1 inch is better handled by loppers or a pruning saw. Pushing the trimmer beyond its rated capacity will wear the blade faster and risks binding, particularly on green, flexible growth.

### How does the EGO HT2411 compare to gas hedge trimmers in the same price range?

At $130 to $150 for the kit, the HT2411 is priced competitively against entry-level gas trimmers. The gas alternatives in this price range, including models like the Husqvarna 122HD45, will have longer run times and slightly more raw cutting power, but they require fuel mixing, seasonal maintenance, and produce exhaust. The EGO starts instantly, runs quietly, and weighs less. For most residential hedge work, the practical difference in cutting performance is small enough that the gas advantages don't justify the maintenance overhead.
