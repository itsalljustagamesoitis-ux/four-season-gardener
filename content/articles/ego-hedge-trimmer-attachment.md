The [EGO POWER+ HTA2000 20" Hedge Trimmer Attachment](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B08LZBQLVT?tag=fourseasong-20) is not a standalone tool. That's the first thing to say, and it's the thing most people skip over before buying. It's an attachment for the EGO Multi-Head Power System, which means you need the PH1400E power head to run it. If you already own one, this attachment is a reasonable buy at around $100 to $120. If you don't own the power head, you're looking at a combined investment closer to $280 to $320 depending on current pricing and where you buy. That changes the math considerably, and any review that doesn't say so upfront is wasting your time.

With that context established: if you run a larger property, keep an eye on the [Battery & Cordless Tools](/battery-equipment/) section of this site, because the EGO multi-head platform has been expanding steadily and it's worth understanding as a system rather than evaluating any single attachment in isolation.

## Quick Verdict

The HTA2000 does what it's designed to do. The telescoping shaft on the PH1400E power head extends your reach without requiring a ladder, the blade tracks cleanly on most hedging material, and the 56V battery shared across the EGO platform means you're not managing separate charging systems for every tool in your shed.

The 20-inch blade is the main limitation. Dedicated pole hedge trimmers from Stihl, including the HLA 56 and HLA 86, run 20 to 24 inches on the blade and are purpose-built for this job. The EGO attachment is a competitive option for anyone already in the EGO ecosystem, but if you're starting from zero and your only need is a pole hedge trimmer, a dedicated unit probably makes more sense. The HTA2000 earns its place as a smart add-on to a platform you're already using, not as a first-time standalone purchase.

![Ego Hedge Trimmer Attachment](/images/articles/battery-equipment-1.jpg)


**Buy it if:** You own the PH1400E and have hedges or topiary above shoulder height that currently require ladder work.

**Skip it if:** You're not in the EGO system and you just need one hedge trimmer. Look at a dedicated unit instead, or consider the full [EGO hedge trimmer with battery and charger](/ego-hedge-trimmer-with-battery-and-charger/) package as a starting point.

## Key Specs

The HTA2000 attachment runs on EGO's 56V ARC Lithium platform. The blade is 20 inches, dual-action, with a 3/4-inch cut capacity. Tooth spacing is approximately 3/4 inch as well, which handles most formal hedging material without difficulty.

The attachment weighs around 4.2 pounds on its own. Once mounted on the PH1400E power head with a 2.5Ah battery, total system weight comes in around 10 to 11 pounds. That's not light, but it's distributed along the shaft rather than concentrated at your wrist, which matters over a two-hour trimming session.

The telescoping shaft extends from approximately 40 inches to 59 inches. The cutting head articulates to 135 degrees, which allows you to work horizontal top surfaces, angled faces, and moderate overhead angles without repositioning constantly.

One spec that doesn't appear prominently in the product listing: the HTA2000 is compatible only with the PH1400E power head, not older EGO multi-head configurations. Check your existing power head model number before ordering.

## Performance and Testing

I ran the HTA2000 through two full trimming sessions on my property: one on a 60-foot run of established arborvitae averaging around 8 feet tall, and one on a mixed hemlock and boxwood border that's more irregular in density. Both sessions used a 5.0Ah 56V battery.

### Reach and Articulation

The reach is the obvious selling point, and it works. Standing flat-footed, I could address the top of an 8-foot arborvitae without difficulty. The 135-degree head articulation sounds like a lot on a spec sheet, but in practice it meant I could flip between the top face and the angled side face of the hedge without disassembling anything or shifting my stance substantially. I did a rough count of ladder trips during a comparable session with my old Husqvarna 125BVx hedge trimmer setup last year: eleven. With the HTA2000, that number was zero. (I timed this.)

![Ego Hedge Trimmer Attachment](/images/articles/battery-equipment-2.jpg)


If you've ever stopped mid-session to reposition a ladder on uneven ground next to a prickly hedge, you know exactly what problem this solves.

### Cut Quality

Clean on arborvitae, acceptable on the hemlock, slower on a section of overgrown boxwood that had some woody stems approaching the 3/4-inch capacity limit. The dual-action blade reduces vibration noticeably compared to single-action alternatives, and the cut face on the arborvitae was as clean as I'd expect from a dedicated corded trimmer. No significant tearing on new growth.

The 20-inch blade length becomes a limitation on wider hedges where you're making multiple passes. A 24-inch blade, like you'd get on the Stihl HLA 135, covers more surface per stroke and reduces pass count on long straight runs. For most residential hedging, 20 inches is fine. For anything over 100 feet of formal hedging at consistent height, the shorter blade will add time.

### Battery and Runtime

On the 5.0Ah battery, I trimmed approximately 180 linear feet of arborvitae (both the top and one side face) before the battery indicator dropped to one bar. That's a reasonable session for most properties. EGO rates the system for up to 75 minutes of runtime, and my experience tracks roughly with that on moderate material. Dense growth or repeated blade loading will pull that number down.

The 56V platform means you can swap to any compatible EGO battery if one runs out, which is a meaningful convenience if you're also running an EGO blower or mower on the same charge day. I cross-reference this more than I expected to.

![Ego Hedge Trimmer Attachment](/images/articles/battery-equipment-3.jpg)


### Noise and Vibration

Measured against a gas pole trimmer, the noise difference is substantial. Against other battery-powered alternatives like the [EGO pole hedge trimmer](/ego-pole-hedge-trimmer/) with its integrated motor, the HTA2000 is comparable. Vibration through the shaft is low. After 90 minutes of use, my forearms were tired from the weight of the tool, not from vibration fatigue. That's a real difference from older equipment.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**

- Eliminates ladder work on hedges up to roughly 9 feet tall from ground level
- 56V platform battery shared across EGO's full tool lineup
- Dual-action blade with 3/4-inch cut capacity handles most residential hedging material cleanly
- Articulating head (135 degrees) handles both top and side faces without tool changes
- Significantly quieter than any gas alternative at this reach

**Cons:**

- PH1400E power head required and sold separately. Total system cost is $280 to $320 if you're starting from scratch
- 20-inch blade is shorter than dedicated pole hedge trimmers from Stihl and other brands
- Combined system weight of 10 to 11 pounds is manageable but not light over long sessions
- Compatibility limited to the PH1400E. Verify your power head before ordering

## Who It's For

The HTA2000 makes obvious sense for one type of buyer: someone already running EGO's multi-head system who has formal hedging above shoulder height. If you already own the PH1400E, the attachment price is around $100 to $120 and the ROI is essentially one session of avoided ladder work and related back complaints.

It also makes sense if you're building out an EGO platform from scratch and hedge trimming is one of several tasks you want to cover. The multi-head system currently supports string trimmer, edger, pole saw, and cultivator attachments in addition to the HTA2000. If you're consolidating away from gas or from a cabinet full of single-battery tools, the shared platform logic is sound. I'd recommend looking at the full suite of [cordless and battery-powered tools](/battery-equipment/) before buying individual attachments to make sure the PH1400E covers the use cases you actually have.

![Ego Hedge Trimmer Attachment](/images/articles/battery-equipment-4.jpg)


It's less compelling as a first and only hedge tool if you have no other EGO equipment. In that case, a dedicated pole hedge trimmer gives you a complete unit, often with a larger blade, at a comparable price point. The Stihl HLA 56 starts around $230 to $250 without battery and runs a 20-inch blade on Stihl's AK platform. The HLA 86 extends reach further and runs around $330 to $350 similarly battery-excluded. Either is purpose-built and arguably better optimized for the single task. The EGO wins on platform flexibility, not on per-task optimization.

One practical note on property scale: if you're managing under 50 feet of formal hedging, a standard corded or handheld unit will likely serve you better regardless of brand. The pole configuration earns its keep on volume and height, not on small amounts of trim work.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need to buy the power head separately, or does the HTA2000 come with one?

Separately. The [EGO POWER+ HTA2000](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B08LZBQLVT?tag=fourseasong-20) is an attachment only. You need the EGO PH1400E Multi-Head Power System power head to run it, and that is sold as a separate purchase. The power head itself runs around $160 to $200, which puts the combined entry cost at approximately $280 to $320 before any battery costs if you don't already own one.

### Which EGO batteries are compatible with the HTA2000 system?

The HTA2000 uses EGO's 56V ARC Lithium battery platform. Any EGO 56V battery will run the system. For most trimming sessions, the 2.5Ah gets you through lighter work and the 5.0Ah is the practical choice for longer runs. Runtime climbs to around 75 minutes on moderate material with the 5.0Ah. Batteries purchased for other EGO tools, including their mowers and blowers, transfer directly.

![Ego Hedge Trimmer Attachment](/images/articles/battery-equipment-5.jpg)


### How does the HTA2000 compare to the Stihl HLA 86 pole hedge trimmer?

The Stihl HLA 86 is a purpose-built pole hedge trimmer with a longer reach and a blade up to 24 inches depending on configuration. It's optimized specifically for this task. The EGO HTA2000 attached to the PH1400E is a multi-purpose platform attachment. If hedge trimming is your primary need and you're not already in the EGO system, the Stihl is arguably the stronger single-task tool. The EGO wins if you want one battery platform running multiple attachments across different jobs.

### What is the maximum reach of the HTA2000 on the PH1400E?

The PH1400E shaft extends from approximately 40 to 59 inches. Add the attachment length and your own height, and most users standing around 5'8" to 6' can comfortably reach hedge surfaces at 9 to 10 feet from ground level. The 135-degree head articulation means the blade face can be positioned horizontal for top trimming without requiring you to hold the shaft nearly vertical.

### Is the HTA2000 compatible with older EGO multi-head power heads?

No. The HTA2000 is designed specifically for the PH1400E. EGO made changes to their multi-head connector design, and older EGO power heads are not compatible with the HTA2000 attachment. Before ordering, confirm your power head model number is PH1400E. This is listed in the product specs and on the EGO website.
