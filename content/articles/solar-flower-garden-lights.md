Solar flower garden lights occupy a strange middle ground in outdoor lighting. They're decorative enough to soften a border or pathway, but the better ones pull real security duty too. The problem is the category is flooded with products that look identical in the listing photos and perform very differently once they're staked into your actual yard and left to charge through a partly cloudy week in November.

I've been testing lights on my property for years, and I've learned that two specs matter more than anything else: waterproofing and lumens. Most listings bury both. I'm going to compare two budget-range solar lights here that serve different purposes and different buyers. One is a ground-stake spotlight, one is a wall-mounted security unit. If you've been browsing the [Garden Lighting](/lighting/) section trying to figure out which direction to go, this should settle it.

The two products are the [LITOM 30 LED Solar Motion Sensor Landscape Spotlights, 4-Pack](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B075GQ54CY?tag=fourseasong-20) and the [Mr Beams Solar Wedge Plus 102 LED Security Outdoor Wall Light](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07W464JPY?tag=fourseasong-20). Neither is expensive. They are not trying to do the same thing.

Both run entirely on solar, require zero wiring, and trigger on motion. The differences show up in placement, installation type, and how much light they actually throw.

## Why Choose the LITOM 30 LED 4-Pack

The [LITOM 30 LED Solar Motion Sensor Landscape Spotlights, 4-Pack](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B075GQ54CY?tag=fourseasong-20) costs around $30 for four units at the time of writing, which works out to roughly $7.50 per light. That's a floor price for outdoor solar with a serious waterproof rating.

![Solar Flower Garden Lights](/images/articles/lighting-7.jpg)


### The IP67 Rating Is the Real Story

Most solar landscape lights come rated IP44 or IP65. IP67 means the units can be submerged in up to one meter of water for 30 minutes. In practical terms, that means a heavy spring downpour, a drainage issue near a border bed, or a lawn that holds standing water after snow melt is not going to kill these lights. IP65 offers splash protection. IP67 offers genuine submersion tolerance. (I've tested IP65 lights that failed after a single wet winter. The rating matters.)

If you've ever pulled up a solar stake light in March and found the battery compartment full of rust and moisture, that's what IP67 prevents.

### Three Modes Give You Actual Flexibility

A lot of solar spotlights offer one mode: motion-activated, and you're done. The LITOM units give you three. Mode one is standard motion-activated bright light. Mode two is permanent dim light with a motion-triggered brightness boost. Mode three is permanent bright light throughout the night.

Mode two is the one I use. A low baseline glow along a front walkway is more useful than a light that sits in complete darkness until something triggers it. If you've ever watched a guest hesitate at the edge of an unlit path because they couldn't see where it started, that's what mode two solves.

### Coverage Math Works in the LITOM's favor

Four lights for $30 means you can line a 20-foot pathway, frame a garden bed, or anchor four separate planting areas without buying multiple single units. For decorative solar flower garden lights used to define borders or highlight plantings, the per-unit cost is hard to beat at this rating level.

The separate solar panel connects to the light unit via a short cable, which means you can position the panel in the sun and angle the light toward the feature you want illuminated, even if those two things aren't in the same spot. That's not a small thing when you're working with partial shade around a mixed border.

### What You Give Up

The built-in rechargeable battery will degrade. Most lithium batteries in solar lights lose meaningful capacity after 500 to 800 charge cycles, which at daily use is somewhere between 18 months and 3 years. Since the battery is not user-replaceable, when it goes, you replace the unit. At $7.50 each, that's tolerable, though I recognize it creates waste that wouldn't exist with a replaceable-cell design.

Placement is also constrained. The solar panel needs direct sun exposure for several hours daily to charge adequately. Shaded garden positions, which are exactly where a lot of decorative plantings live, will give you poor run time.

## Why Choose the Mr Beams Solar Wedge Plus

The [Mr Beams Solar Wedge Plus 102 LED Security Outdoor Wall Light](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07W464JPY?tag=fourseasong-20) runs around $35 per unit and is a different product category from the LITOM, even though both respond to motion and run on solar. The Wedge Plus is a wall light. It goes on a vertical surface. It doesn't stake into the ground.

### 410 Lumens Is Legitimately Bright for a Solar Unit

The manufacturer rates the Wedge Plus at 410 lumens. To put that in context, many solar motion lights in this price range produce 100 to 200 lumens. The LITOM doesn't publish a lumen figure at all, which tells you something. At 410 lumens, the Wedge Plus throws enough light to illuminate a garage entrance, a side gate, or a back door clearly enough to be useful rather than merely decorative.

If security lighting is part of your requirement, rather than pure decoration, 410 lumens is the threshold where solar starts doing real work. You can read our breakdown of dedicated [best security lights outdoor](/best-security-lights-outdoor/) options if that's your primary need rather than aesthetics, but the Wedge Plus holds its own in mixed use.

![Solar Flower Garden Lights](/images/articles/lighting-1.jpg)


### Installation Takes About Four Minutes

The solar panel is built into the top of the unit. There's no separate panel, no cable to route, no secondary mounting decision. You hold it against the wall, mark two screw holes, drill, mount. (I timed a similar Mr Beams unit once. It was faster than writing this sentence about it.)

For anyone renting, this matters. Two screw holes in a wall surface are a quick patch job when you move out. No wiring, no conduit, no junction box. This is also the better answer for anyone who wants to avoid committing to a permanent installation while they figure out what lighting layout actually works on their property.

This makes the Wedge Plus comparable to a hardwired option like the LEONLITE LED Security Floodlight in terms of the security result, but with zero electrical work involved.

### The Integrated Panel Creates a Placement Constraint

Because the solar panel is on top of the unit and fixed, you can't adjust it independently of the light direction. The wall where you mount it needs to receive direct sun from above. A wall facing north won't charge the unit adequately. A wall under a wide eave may not either, depending on the roof angle. The LITOM's separate panel gives you more options in that specific situation.

The Wedge Plus is also motion-activated only. There's no dim mode, no permanent-on setting. It stays dark until something crosses its sensor range. For a security application on a back wall or garage, that's fine. For decorative or pathway lighting where you want continuous low light, it's limiting.

Smart home integration isn't available. If you've built your outdoor lighting around a connected system, this unit won't participate in it.

## Verdict

These two products are not direct competitors. Buying the wrong one for your situation is more of a planning failure than a product failure.

![Solar Flower Garden Lights](/images/articles/lighting-2.jpg)


Buy the [LITOM 30 LED Solar Motion Sensor Landscape Spotlights, 4-Pack](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B075GQ54CY?tag=fourseasong-20) if you want ground-stake landscape spotlights to line a path, frame flower beds, or highlight specific plantings at a low per-unit cost. The IP67 rating is the standout specification in this price range, the three-mode operation adds real flexibility, and four units for $30 makes sense if you need coverage across a longer frontage. The separate panel gives you positioning options that an integrated panel doesn't.

At 410 lumens, it's the brighter unit by a meaningful margin. It suits a garage wall, side entrance, or back door better than it suits a flower bed.

If your priority is decorating a garden border with lights that complement plantings, the LITOM is the right direction. If your priority is a working no-commitment wall light that also happens to enhance the exterior of the property, the Wedge Plus earns its place. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use the LITOM spotlights in a shaded garden area?

Partially shaded is workable, but the solar panel needs at least four to six hours of direct sun daily to maintain a useful charge. The panel connects via cable, so you can position it away from the light unit, in a sunnier spot, which helps. A fully shaded mounting location won't give adequate run time regardless of the cable positioning.

![Solar Flower Garden Lights](/images/articles/lighting-3.jpg)


### How long does the Mr Beams Wedge Plus stay on after detecting motion?

The motion-activated runtime is approximately 30 seconds after the last motion detected in the sensor field. Sensitivity and range can vary by ambient temperature and the specific sensor angle. At lower temperatures in hard winters, battery performance in most solar units decreases, which can shorten activation times.

### Are either of these lights bright enough to deter intruders?

The Mr Beams Wedge Plus at 410 lumens is a credible deterrent light, comparable to a low-end hardwired security fixture. The LITOM units don't publish a lumen output, and based on their primary decorative and pathway use case, they're not positioned as security lighting. For a dedicated security application, the Wedge Plus is the stronger choice of the two, though our [best security lights outdoor](/best-security-lights-outdoor/) article covers purpose-built options if that's the main goal.

### What happens to these lights in heavy rain or snow?

The LITOM 4-Pack carries an IP67 rating, meaning it can handle submersion up to one meter for 30 minutes. Rain and snow are not a concern. The Mr Beams Wedge Plus does not carry a published IP rating. The manufacturer describes it as weather-resistant, but that's a marketing description rather than a tested standard. For genuinely wet climates or positions exposed to snow accumulation, the LITOM's IP67 certification is the more reliable specification.

### Can I add more LITOM units later if I want to extend my pathway coverage?

Yes. The LITOM units are sold in multi-packs, and individual units operate independently. Adding more units later means buying another pack, which currently runs around $30 for four. Each unit has its own solar panel, battery, and sensor, so there's no pairing or syncing required.
